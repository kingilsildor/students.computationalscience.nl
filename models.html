Traffic Models

To see how complex systems like traffic emerge, we modeled traffic flow through a roundabout in a city. For this image the cars are the red squared, whereby the roundabout is the centre brown square. This model was made as simple as possible, by only using local rules. The following rules were defined:

  • Cars will try to move at maximum speed on straight roads.
  • Cars will stop when a car is in front of them.
  • Cars will move on the rotary until the flag is changed to exit.
Hereby the following assumptions were made:
  • Cars can move instantly to full speed and will break instantly.
  • Cars don't have a predefined destination.
  • Cars can only move as far as they can, never overtaking another car.
Besides these simple rules, we could still observere that the density of cars made a big difference on traffic jams, as one would expect from real life. Hereby the change in speed limits didn't change that much.

COVID-19 Exposure in Grocery Stores

During COVID it was mandantory to walk certain routes within supermarkets. As a project we modelled if this was indeed more efficient to dicrease the spreading of COVID, compared to normal paths. For this we used an Agent-Based Modeling approach, in which agents moved across a custom-designed network. The assumption was made that only a certain amount of agents could occupy a single node. The walking paths were designed based on the shopping list of the customers, with some noise added so that the agents could walk back and forward. How darker the node, how higher the probability to get infected.

SIR Model

The SIR model is a simple and widely used epidemiological model that describes how a disease spreads through a fixed population. It divides the population into three compartments: Susceptible (S), Infected (I), and Recovered (R). Individuals move from being susceptible to infected when they come into contact with an infected person, with a transmission rate of β and they recover at a rate of γ, after which they become immune.
It is a simple but effective model and it was used a lot during COVID, because it can capture the key dynamics. But given the nature of the model, every person is contacted with every other person in the population. Something that doesn't happen in real life. Besides it is a deterministic model, meaning that based on the initial parameters we can show how the system evolves. Given that real life is non deterministic, this can couse some problems.
This is one of the first models you would learn when participating this master program.
