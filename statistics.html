On this page you can find various statistics of the student cohort, ranging from general statistics to topics surrounding the program and life as a student here. These statistics were obtained from a survey sent out to all CLS students currently enrolled, of which 48 responded.

General Stats

The following three graphs provide an overview of the students' ages, nationalities and previous studies. All, except for two students, stated that they have some previous work experience as well. Forty percent of all responses were from women.

Program Related

When asked what they appreciate about the program, the most common response was its broad nature. They value that we learn a set of skills that can be applied in various domains and that students have flexibility when it comes to choosing their courses. Images 4 and 6 show some insight into the student's study choices. Another regular response was to show appreciation for their classmates, teachers and staff. The students seem to like that there is diversity in nationalities and in academic backgrounds. There were multiple comments on the professors being knowledgeable, and the staff being helpful.

What seems to be a commonly appreciated thing about the master program is its broad nature. Students appreciate that the program covers various domains and that they have flexibitiy when it comes to choosing classes. They note that the skillset we learn is applicable in real life.
Another thing that the students appreciate are the people in the program, both students and staff. They like the diversity in their collegues, that the professors seem motivated and knowledgable, and that the staff is kind and helpful.

Life Related

We asked the students about their experience with housing and if they had any interesting stories. About half of the responses stated that they had received very expensive offers. A few of these students still ended up accepting the offer, potentially from a lack of better options.
