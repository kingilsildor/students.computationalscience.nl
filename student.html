Diverse horizons and evolving ambitions define the Master of Computational Science cohort. Some of these students will pick courses related to their original field. Whereby others will get out of their comfort zone during the program. In this chapter we will let two of the studens tell their story.

Zainab Nasser

As I approach the end of my first year in the Master’s in Computational Science at the University of Amsterdam, I am genuinely impressed by how much I’ve learned, the connections I’ve made, and how inspired I feel by the work being done here. I completed my undergraduate degree in Mathematics at Purdue University in the U.S. and began working as an Actuarial Analyst afterwards. While the work was rewarding and meaningful, I felt a stronger pull towards academia and interdisciplinary research. During a semester abroad in Den Haag during my final year of my Bachelor’s, I become familiar with the education system in the Netherlands. Many students told me very positive things about the research opportunities and education at the University of Amsterdam which sparked my curiosity.

While searching for different programs that would combine my interests in mathematics, science, and research, I came across the relatively new Computational Science program here at UvA. Along with the interdisciplinary nature that immediately stood out to me, I also noticed that those who graduated from this program were able to go on to pursue careers in industry, research, or PhD programs.

Since the moment I started this program, my confidence in my decision has only grown. The curriculum allows you to explore various computational applications across fields like finance, physics, biology, chemistry, high performance computing, with a lot of flexibility in the courses you take. Students can also take classes at the Vrije Universiteit, which adds so much more flexibility to which courses you can take and what you can learn here.

Most importantly, the community and learning environment in the program is welcoming and very collaborative. Since the students can come from all types of academic backgrounds from all over the world, I really feel like I never stop learning from the students around me. Professors are approachable and passionate about their work, and it seems like they’re genuinely interested in our education and growth. The program is challenging and has pushed me outside of my comfort zone, but even those moments have been incredibly rewarding

Timo Nijkamp

Hi! If you're reading this, you're probably interested in the Master's in Computational Science at the University of Amsterdam and looking for some insider insights. I’d be happy to help! My name is Timo, and I’m currently in the final two months of my first year of this two-year programme. I completed a Bachelor's in Economics and did a minor in Computer Programming at Science Park. Economics provided me with a solid foundation in mathematics and statistics, while my minor taught me essential programming skills. Altogether, this background allowed me to meet the entry requirements for the Master's, and hence why I applied. A few weeks later I heard I got accepted. Great!

On the UvA website, the Computational Science programme is described as follows: "In the Master's programme Computational Science you learn to develop computational models based on observations and data from physical, social, earth, economic and life sciences." When people ask what I study, I often start with something similar. As you progress through the programme, you'll find this description is accurate. Though it can sound a bit abstract to outsiders and if you give them this description you are often met with confused faces. In those cases, I usually say: “I basically do math, but instead of solving it myself, I let the computer figure it out. Oh, and also some AI.” For me, this sums it up nicely, especially since I’ve chosen a specialisation in Computational Finance and taken most of my electives in AI.

So, how do I find the Master's so far? I’m really enjoying it. First of all, the community is great. Almost everyone in our year is actively involved and knows each other through either group projects, lectures, or our famously exclusive Master's drinks. Second, coming from an economics background, I’m learning a lot of new things. At first, I was a bit concerned about the level, but don’t worry, If you are willing to invest some time and effort, all courses are more than doable and great fun. Third, the freedom this programme offers is fantastic. It’s one of the most customizable Master’s in the Netherlands, making it ideal for exploring a wide range of topics. In my case, that has included brain imaging, finance, complex systems, and machine learning. I love this flexibility, it allows me to build a broad skill set while diving into new areas that interest me.

In conclusion, if you enjoy working on complex problems using both mathematics and programming in many different fields, this Master’s is a great fit. It’s a programme that challenges you, while offering a super fun and collaborative environment to grow and learn in.
